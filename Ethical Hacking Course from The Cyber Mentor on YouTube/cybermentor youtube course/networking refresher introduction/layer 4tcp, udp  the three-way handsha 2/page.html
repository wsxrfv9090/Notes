transport layer of the OSI model
TCP: Transmission Control Protocol
its a connection oriented protocol
this protocol is best suited for higher reliability

UDP: User Datagram Protocol
its a connectionless protocol

Scanning both tcp and udp as a penetration tester:

Usually you just use TCP for scanning, and scanning is a important process

TCP works on what is called a three-way handshake
Three-way handshakes:
1. Send out a SYN packet
2. Recieve back a SYN ACT packet
3. Last send a ACT packet

explain:
think of it as an iteraction, like you have a friend or a neighber, you go to your neighber and say hello, that's sending a SYN, then your friend wave back that's receiving a back a SYN ACT packet, then you know you can start making conversations so you send your acknowledge, meaning you are sending back a ACT backet.

PORTS:
Different protocols have different ports: for ex, http is over port 80, https is over port 443

the process of getting connected to a website for example

and if port 443 is available and opeen for connection,
